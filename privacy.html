Privacy Policy

Effective: 29 June 2026

TL;DR: ARCO stores everything locally on your device. There is no server, no analytics, no tracking, no ad networks, no data sold to anyone. Apple handles your payment. That's it.

1. Who runs ARCO

ARCO is an independent iOS app built by Thinh Tran. Contact: thinht7110@gmail.com

2. What data ARCO collects

ARCO does not have a backend server. All data you enter into the app — habits, tasks, focus sessions, schedules, app blocklists, journal entries — is stored locally on your iPhone using Apple's SwiftData framework. The data never leaves your device unless you explicitly back it up via iCloud as part of your iPhone backup, which is managed by Apple under their privacy policy.

ARCO collects no personally identifiable information. No email, no name, no phone number, no IP address, no device identifiers are sent anywhere by the app.

3. Screen Time and Family Controls

ARCO uses Apple's Screen Time API (Family Controls + Managed Settings) to enforce the app blocker. When you grant Screen Time permission, iOS gives ARCO the ability to shield (block) the specific apps you select. ARCO does not see which apps you use, how long you use them, or what you do inside them — Apple keeps that data private. ARCO only knows the opaque tokens for the apps you chose to block.

The Bad Habits / Insights screen displays usage statistics that come directly from iOS Screen Time and are computed on-device by Apple's DeviceActivityReport extension. ARCO never sees or transmits this data.

4. Subscriptions and payment

ARCO offers an optional subscription. Payment is handled entirely by Apple through the App Store. ARCO does not receive your credit card number, billing address, or any other payment information. RevenueCat (a subscription analytics service) processes subscription receipts to verify entitlements. RevenueCat receives an anonymous user ID generated by ARCO — no personal information. See RevenueCat's privacy policy.

5. Notifications

If you grant notification permission, ARCO schedules local notifications (habit reminders, focus session alerts). These notifications are created and delivered entirely on your device. They are never sent to any server.

6. Analytics and tracking

ARCO does not use any analytics SDK (no Firebase, no Mixpanel, no Amplitude, etc.). ARCO does not use any advertising network. ARCO does not implement App Tracking Transparency tracking because there is nothing to track.

7. Crash reports

ARCO does NOT collect crash reports. If iOS itself prompts you to share diagnostics with developers, Apple may include anonymized crash logs in those reports under their own privacy policy.

8. Children

ARCO is rated 4+ in the App Store and is not directed at children under 13. ARCO does not knowingly collect any data from anyone.

9. Your rights

Because ARCO does not collect or store your data on any server, there is no data to request, export, or delete on our side. To remove all ARCO data, delete the app from your iPhone — that wipes the local database completely.

10. Changes to this policy

If this policy changes materially, the new version will be posted at this URL with an updated effective date. Continued use of ARCO after a change means you accept the updated policy.

11. Contact

Questions about privacy? Email thinht7110@gmail.com — usually a reply within 24 hours.
