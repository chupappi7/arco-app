Common questions

How does the app blocker work?

ARCO uses Apple's Screen Time API (Family Controls + Managed Settings) to enforce blocks. You pick which apps to block, then start a focus session — those apps will be shielded until you stop. ARCO never sees what you actually do inside the blocked apps — Apple keeps that private. Just the blocking is enforced.

Why does the app ask for Screen Time permission?

To block apps during focus sessions, iOS requires you to grant Screen Time access. This is a one-time permission. You can revoke it anytime in iOS Settings → Screen Time → Family Controls.

Does ARCO collect my data?

No. ARCO stores all your habits, tasks, and focus sessions locally on your device. There's no cloud backend, no analytics, no tracking. See the privacy policy for details.

Will my data sync across devices?

Not yet. iCloud sync is on the roadmap. Today all data is local to the device.

How do I cancel my subscription?

iOS Settings → tap your name at the top → Subscriptions → ARCO → Cancel Subscription. The cancellation takes effect at the end of the current billing period.

I was charged but the app says I'm not subscribed.

Open ARCO → Settings → Restore Purchases. This re-syncs your subscription from your Apple ID. If that doesn't fix it, email me with your purchase receipt and I'll resolve it within 24 hours.

The Focus Live Activity won't appear on my lock screen.

iOS Settings → Notifications → ARCO → Allow Live Activities (must be on). Also check that "Always On Display" or focus mode isn't suppressing widgets.
